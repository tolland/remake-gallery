# Remake Gallery

**Remake Gallery** is a personal portfolio of visual reproductions — a curated collection of artworks, designs, and creative experiments that I’ve recreated by hand or in code.
Each piece is a **remake**, not an original — but the act of reproduction is itself a kind of study. By imitating works that resonate with me, I explore what makes them compelling: composition, color, structure, rhythm, or code.

---

## 🎨 Concept

The idea for *Remake Gallery* was inspired by the story of the **Prado Mona Lisa** — a painting long dismissed as a copy, until modern imaging revealed that its underdrawing matched Leonardo’s *Mona Lisa* stroke for stroke.
It turned out not to be a forgery at all, but a **contemporaneous study**, likely painted by one of Leonardo’s students alongside the master himself.

That discovery reframed the idea of copying:
> A reproduction can be an act of learning, reverence, and insight — not imitation, but participation.

---

## 🧩 Purpose

- To **deconstruct** works that attract me — visually, conceptually, or technically.
- To **understand** their mechanics through reconstruction.
- To **document** what I learn in the process.

Each project in this gallery includes:
- The original reference or inspiration (when known)
- My remake or interpretation
- Notes on tools, techniques, and lessons learned

---

## ⚙️ Stack / Tools

Depending on the project:
- 🐍 **Python**, **TypeScript**, **Kotlin**, or creative code frameworks
- 🖌️ Graphic or 3D tools like **Three.js**, **Godot**, or **Flutter Canvas**
- 🧠 Concepts borrowed from design systems, generative art, and UI replication

---

## 📚 Ethos

This project isn’t about originality — it’s about **practice through precision**.
In a world obsessed with novelty, there’s value in *reconstruction* — in understanding how beauty or utility is built.
Much like the Prado student tracing Leonardo’s strokes, I’m tracing the logic and feel of things that work.

---
